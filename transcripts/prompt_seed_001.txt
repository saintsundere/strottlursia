The bar was the kind of place that didn't try. That was the thing about it — no effort had been made and somehow that was the effort, the deliberate withholding of polish that produced a specific warmth, low-ceilinged and yellow-lit, where everyone present had arrived at the same unspoken agreement to be unremarkable together for a few hours. Someone was telling a story at the far end. Nobody was listening completely but everybody was listening a little, the way you do when a story is background music you've chosen not to identify. The woman beside him had her coat still on, not because she was cold, because she'd forgotten. He could tell by the way she was leaning, entirely present, entirely inside whatever she was thinking, the coat just something that had arrived with her and stayed. He thought: this is it. Not a thought about her specifically. Just — this. The room. The yellow light. The story nobody was fully listening to. The warmth was structural, load-bearing, shared without anyone having decided to share it. He held his glass. He didn't drink from it. He was trying to stay inside the moment long enough to feel it from the outside too, which he knew even as he tried it was the thing that would end it — and it did, a minute later, or maybe five, the warmth just gone, the room reassembling itself into its parts, the story finishing or not finishing, the woman beside him shifting her weight, still in her coat, the glass in his hand now just cold.
