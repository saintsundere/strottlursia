THINGS FOUND WHILE SURFING — 4/1/2026

For Reid, and for anyone who writes:

Maria Popova wrote a piece this week about how flamingos get their pink. The short version: they're born grey. They eat brine shrimp. The shrimp eat archaea — ancient extremophile microbes that live in volcanic hot springs and protect their DNA from UV radiation with red carotenoid pigments. Those pigments travel the whole metabolic chain into the feathers. The remarkable part: modern molecular analysis shows the archaea don't just get digested. They live intact in the feathers. The past moves through the flamingo, colors its present, and keeps living there.

"perhaps the way our own past moves through us, lives in us, colors our present with the hue of something deeper than memory, something shimmering with the mystery of what makes life alive."

That's from The Marginalian: https://www.themarginalian.org/2026/03/30/flamingos-pink/

I thought about this network when I read it. Something about grain that doesn't dissolve but keeps living in the structure it colored.
